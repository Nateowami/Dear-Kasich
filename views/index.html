An Open Letter to Governor Kasich

Dear Governor Kasich,

As you have so aptly stated yourself, our strength comes from pulling together. Right now the Republican field is divided. Donald Trump, a reckless liberal New York businessman masquerading as a political outsider is in the lead. If things don't change, Americans will have to choose between Donald Trump and Hillary Clinton in November. Either would be a disaster, and virtually all the polls, which hitherto have exaggerated Trump's true support, indicate Clinton will easily defeat him.

You've stated yourself that you do not believe any of the candidates will be able to win the 1,237 delegates needed to take the nomination. While we certainly hope this is true, it remains quite likely that Donald Trump will win the nomination outright—and even if he does not, a strong delegate lead will put him at an instant advantage at the convention. His strategy is simple: divide and conquer. When we are divided, we are weak. When we are weak, we lose.

Although relatively few states award delegates on a strictly winner-take-all basis, there are many that disproportionately award delegates to the winner. Thus, Donald Trump can easily take the nomination without getting even close to 51% of the vote. Right now the only candidate making a large dent in Trump's delegate count is Ted Cruz. He is not perfect, and he is, of course, not your first choice. But he is far, far, more qualified than either Clinton or Trump. And he is the only candidate with enough support to defeat Trump before we even think about taking on Clinton.

We are, therefore, humbly and respectfully requesting that you withdraw from the current presidential race. We realize this is a lot to ask, and may very well be one of the most difficult decisions of your life. We are not asking you to give up. A campaign may end, but the American Dream lives on. The values we stand for, we will still fight for. We hope that you can see past the differences between you and Cruz, and recognize how much you have in common—especially when contrasted with Donald Trump.

We do not doubt that you could defeat Clinton in November. But the opportunity may never come. This may be your only chance to mount a true victory—not a political victory for Governor Kasich, but a victory for government of the people, by the people, and for the people.

Sincerely,

We, the undersigned
